How to estimate?
Moisture must be between 0 and 100.
Method 1: The "Feel" Test
Dig about 4-6 inches deep, grab a handful of soil, and squeeze it:
• 10-30%: Dry, crumbles completely, won't form a ball.
• 40-60%: Forms a loose ball. Feels slightly damp.
• 70-80%: Forms a tight ball, leaves a damp outline on hand.
• 90-100%: Muddy. Water drips out when squeezed.

Method 2: The Wooden Stick Test
Insert a wooden chopstick or dowel 4-6 inches into the soil, leave for 5 minutes, then pull it out:
• Dry (<30%): Stick comes out clean.
• Moist (40-70%): Stick comes out dark and slightly damp.
• Wet (>80%): Stick comes out muddy or with water clinging to it.

Method 3: The Weight Method (Most Accurate)
1. Weigh a sample of fresh soil to get the Wet Weight.
2. Dry the soil completely (e.g., in the sun or baked at a low temperature).
3. Weigh it again to get the Dry Weight.
4. Calculate: ((Wet Weight - Dry Weight) / Dry Weight) × 100 to find your exact moisture percentage.
