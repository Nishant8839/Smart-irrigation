Step-by-Step Guide: Measuring Soil Moisture for Farming

This method will help you determine when to irrigate based on soil moisture percentage.

🔹 What You Need

  • ✔ Soil Sample (collected from the farm)
  • ✔ Weighing Scale (preferably digital, accuracy of at least 0.1g)
  • ✔ Hot Plate / Electric Tawa (or low-heat gas stove with aluminum foil)
  • ✔ Metal Pan / Aluminum Tray
  • ✔ Aluminum Foil (to prevent contamination)
  • ✔ Spoon / Metal Rod (for stirring)
  • ✔ Notebook & Pen (to record weights)

🔹 Step 1: Collect Soil Samples

📍 Choose 3-5 locations in your field.
📍 Dig 5-10 cm deep (for shallow crops) or 15-30 cm deep (for deep-rooted crops).
📍 Take about 100–200 grams of soil per sample.
📍 Place each sample in a separate labeled container or plastic bag.

🔹 Step 2: Weigh the Wet Soil (Initial Weight, W₁)

1️⃣ Take a clean, dry container (plate, metal tray, or aluminum foil).
2️⃣ Weigh the empty container and write down its weight (W₀).
3️⃣ Put the wet soil sample into the container.
4️⃣ Weigh again and record the total weight (W₁ = Wet Soil + Container).

🔹 Step 3: Dry the Soil

(A) Using a Hot Plate / Electric Tawa (Recommended)

  • ✅ Set to low heat (50–80°C) to avoid burning.
  • ✅ Place the soil in a thin layer (1–2 cm thick) on an aluminum foil-lined tray.
  • ✅ Stir every 5–10 minutes to ensure even drying.
  • ✅ Dry for 1–2 hours, checking weight every 30 minutes until it stops decreasing.

🔹 Step 4: Weigh the Dry Soil (Final Weight, W₂)

📍 Once dry, let the soil cool in a dry place (not humid air).
📍 Weigh the container + dried soil (W₂).

🔹 Step 5: Calculate Soil Moisture Percentage

Use this formula:

Moisture % = ((W₁ - W₂) / (W₂ - W₀)) × 100

🔹 Example Calculation

Given Data:

Empty container W₀ = 200g
Container + wet soil W₁ = 350g
Container + dry soil W₂ = 300g

Step 1: Calculate Wet & Dry Soil Weights

Wet Soil = W₁ - W₀ = 350g - 200g = 150g
Dry Soil = W₂ - W₀ = 300g - 200g = 100g
